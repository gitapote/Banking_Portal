Accepting Deposits

One of the primary functions of a bank is to accept deposits from individuals, businesses, and other entities. These deposits can include savings accounts, checking accounts, certificates of deposit (CDs), and other types of accounts.

Payment Processing

Banks facilitate transactions and payments between individuals, businesses, and other entities. They provide services such as electronic funds transfers, wire transfers, check processing, credit card processing, and online banking platforms.

Risk Management

Banks are responsible for managing various types of risks, including credit risk, interest rate risk, liquidity risk, and operational risk. They employ risk management practices and regulatory compliance measures to mitigate these risks and ensure the safety and stability of the banking system.
